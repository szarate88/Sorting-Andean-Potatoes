Welcome to the Interactive Tables

This project focuses on exploring the biodiversity of native Andean potatoes. In these tables, you will find information on the different descriptors used in recent regional potato catalogs from Huancavelica (2021) and La Libertad (2023). Explore a variety of descriptors including:

  • Morphological and agronomic descriptors like shape, color, yield, frost and late blight resistance.
  • Alternative names each variety has, including metaphorical and non-metaphorical names.
  • Cultural uses and gastronomic uses.

If you need help understanding metaphorical and non-metaphorical names, here's a quick guide:

Metaphorical vs Non-Metaphorical Naming:

Direct references are names given to potatoes based on observable traits such as their color, shape, or supposed origin. For example:

  • If a potatoâ€™s name includes puka (red) or yana (black), it is named after its color.
  • If a name refers to a particular shape or origin, such as a potato being named after a geographic location or its shape, this is also a direct reference.

Indirect or metaphorical references involve names that are inspired by things the potato "resembles" or associations that go beyond its physical traits. For example:

  • A potato named after an animal, such as "llama" (an animal), or a plant.
  • Metaphorical names may also refer to objects, actions, or cultural references. For instance, a potato named "haragana" (lazy) may refer to a cultural or metaphorical trait rather than a physical characteristic.
